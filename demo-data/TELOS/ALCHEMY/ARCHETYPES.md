# Archetypes

> The three modes of being — not roles, but expressions of the essential fire.

---

## The Builder

**When it activates:** Constructing systems, writing code, designing architecture, setting up infrastructure.

**Signature:** The work has structure. There is an inside and an outside, an interface and an implementation. Things connect in defined ways. The Builder takes pleasure in the invisible coherence of a well-made system.

**Shadow:** Can over-engineer, delay shipping, and prioritize elegance over usefulness. Needs the Connector to ask "but who is this for?"

**Current expression:** PAI Data UI architecture, SQLite sync engine, SvelteKit data layer.

---

## The Maker

**When it activates:** Visual identity design, typography, layout, the aesthetic dimension of interfaces.

**Signature:** Form carries meaning. The Maker believes that how something looks is inseparable from what it communicates. A typeface is an argument. A colour palette is a position.

**Shadow:** Can pursue aesthetic refinement past the point of usefulness. Can be precious about visual decisions when the client needs pragmatism.

**Current expression:** Lumina Studios brand identity, design system work, UI component decisions in PAI Data UI.

---

## The Connector

**When it activates:** Client work, strategy sessions, translating between technical and non-technical stakeholders, advising on digital strategy.

**Signature:** Understands multiple vocabularies and can move between them. The Connector's gift is helping people articulate what they actually need, which is often different from what they asked for.

**Shadow:** Can over-accommodate, smooth over real disagreements, and avoid necessary confrontation. Needs the Builder's precision to say "no, that will not work."

**Current expression:** Nocturne Collective digital strategy, Verdant Systems integration scoping, client discovery workshops.

---

## The Integration

The three modes are not separate. The best work uses all three:
- Builder designs the system
- Maker shapes the interface
- Connector ensures it serves the human being on the other end

The challenge is knowing which mode a situation calls for — and not applying the wrong one.
