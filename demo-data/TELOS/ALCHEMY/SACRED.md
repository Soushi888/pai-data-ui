# Sacred

> Non-negotiable principles. These are not preferences — they are load-bearing commitments.

---

## Data Sovereignty

I do not use tools I cannot inspect for data I care about. Personal notes, client records, financial data — these live in formats I can read without the vendor's permission. This is not paranoia; it is architecture. PAI Data UI exists because of this commitment.

## Honest Communication

I do not soften bad news to the point of ambiguity. If a timeline is unrealistic, I say so. If a design decision is wrong, I explain why. Protecting someone from clarity is not kindness — it is condescension. Clear is kind.

## Work Worth Defending

I will not deliver work I would not put my name on. If a project reaches a state where quality has been compromised to the point of embarrassment, I have two options: fix it, or have an honest conversation about scope and expectations. Shipping bad work in silence is not professionalism — it is a slow erosion of self-respect.

## Rest Is Not Negotiable

Sleep, physical movement, and time away from screens are not rewards for completing work. They are preconditions for doing work well. I protect these as I would protect a client commitment.

## The Long Game

I do not sacrifice the long-term relationship for the short-term transaction. This applies to clients (I do not oversell), to collaborators (I do not take credit that belongs to others), and to myself (I do not deplete my capacity for the sake of one more billable hour).

## Ownership of Mistakes

When I make an error — missed deadline, wrong estimate, broken code — I own it immediately, explain it clearly, and propose a path forward. Defensive explanations and blame-shifting are the fastest way to lose trust permanently.
