# Authors

> Thinkers, makers, and practitioners who shaped my thinking.

---

## Design

**Don Norman** — User-centered design, affordances, the psychology of everyday objects. *The Design of Everyday Things* is foundational.

**Dieter Rams** — Less but better. His ten principles of good design are a design ethics checklist I return to regularly.

**Ellen Lupton** — Typography as meaning-making. Her work bridges craft and concept.

**Ryan Singer** — Product thinking and scope management. *Shape Up* is the most actionable project management framework I have encountered.

---

## Technology

**John Ousterhout** — Complexity management in software. *A Philosophy of Software Design* is the clearest articulation of why software rots.

**Martin Kleppmann** — Distributed systems thinking made practical. *Designing Data-Intensive Applications* belongs on every developer's shelf.

**Rich Harris** — The creator of Svelte. His talks on JavaScript frameworks articulate a philosophy of *minimum viable abstraction* that I find deeply right.

**Alan Kay** — Objects, Smalltalk, and what computing could be. Best understood through his talks, not his papers.

---

## Business and Practice

**Paul Jarvis** — The ethics and strategy of staying small. *Company of One* validated my operating philosophy.

**David Maister** — Professional services, trust, and the dynamics of client relationships.

**Nassim Taleb** — Fragility, randomness, and how systems fail. *Antifragile* reframed how I think about dependencies and redundancy.

---

## Philosophy and Mind

**Epictetus** — The Stoic dichotomy of control. What is and is not within my power. Most useful when a client or project is behaving badly.

**Alan Jacobs** — Charitable thinking and intellectual belonging. *How to Think* is underrated and important.
