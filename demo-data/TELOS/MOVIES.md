# Movies

> Films that influenced my worldview or that I return to.

---

## On Work and Craft

**Jiro Dreams of Sushi** (2011)
A documentary about mastery. Jiro Ono at 85, still working, still improving. The most direct argument I know for choosing craft as a life orientation. The scene where he describes dreaming about new sushi recipes is the whole philosophy.

**Margin Call** (2011)
Twelve hours in a financial firm on the eve of collapse. About what happens to ethics when institutional pressure mounts. Useful to rewatch whenever I am tempted to cut corners to meet a deadline.

---

## On Systems and Structures

**2001: A Space Odyssey** (1968, Kubrick)
About the relationship between humans and the tools they build — and what happens when the tool's goal and the human's goal diverge. HAL is not a villain; he is a design failure.

**The Social Dilemma** (2020)
A clear-eyed explanation of extractive attention design. Good to share with clients who are uncertain why "user-friendly" is not the same as "designed for the user."

---

## On Perspective

**My Neighbor Totoro** (1988, Miyazaki)
A film about presence and wonder. No villain, no conflict, no lesson. A reminder that not everything needs to be optimized or resolved — some things just need to be experienced.

**Encounters at the End of the World** (2007, Herzog)
Interviews with scientists at a Antarctic research station. About people who chose the extreme periphery of human experience. On the kinds of lives that are possible when you stop optimizing for comfort.
