# Music

> Musical life — what I listen to, what I have studied, what moves me.

---

## What I Listen To

**For deep work:** Ambient, minimal techno, Brian Eno, Jon Hopkins, Max Richter. Music with structure but no vocals — the rhythm sets pace without pulling attention.

**For energy:** Post-rock (Explosions in the Sky, Mogwai), jazz (Miles Davis, Bill Evans, Coltrane's *A Love Supreme*), and occasionally African rhythms when I need something that moves differently.

**For thinking:** Silence, or classical guitar. Francisco Tárrega, Leo Brouwer. Single instrument, no arrangement — it clears the workspace.

---

## Practice

I play classical guitar at an amateur level. Not a performer — a practitioner. 20-30 minutes in the evening when the day's work is done. It is the one activity that is purely for itself, with no output, no deliverable, and no client.

The discipline of learning a piece bar by bar, slower than feels productive, until it becomes automatic — this is a useful model for how all skill acquisition works.

---

## What Music Teaches

- Rhythm is a container. What happens inside the container is expression; what defines the container is craft.
- Improvisation requires deeply knowing the rules you are departing from. You cannot play freely with what you have not internalized.
- Some pieces need to be listened to twenty times before they open. Patience with art trains patience with people.
