# Predictions

> Forecasts with confidence levels and time horizons. Revisited annually.

---

## Technology

**Local-first tools will become mainstream for knowledge workers (3-5 years, 75% confidence)**
As privacy concerns grow and SaaS fatigue increases, tools that store data locally and sync optionally will gain significant market share among professional users. Obsidian's growth is an early signal.

**LLM assistance will become a commodity, craft will differentiate (2-3 years, 85% confidence)**
Within 3 years, AI-assisted code and design will be table stakes. The premium will shift to people who can frame problems, evaluate outputs, and integrate work coherently. Prompt engineering as a rare skill will be short-lived.

**Browser-based SQLite will enable a new class of local-first web apps (2-4 years, 65% confidence)**
WASM SQLite running in the browser, combined with sync primitives, will make it practical to build fully offline-capable web applications that own their data. This will challenge the "everything in the cloud" default.

---

## Work and Economy

**Solo practitioners and micro-agencies will continue to outperform large agencies for creative work (ongoing, 80% confidence)**
Large agencies have structural overhead that prevents the depth and responsiveness clients increasingly want. The talent is there; the organizational form is wrong.

**Remote-first will become a durable norm for technical and creative roles (ongoing, 90% confidence)**
The productivity data and talent access arguments for remote work are too strong to reverse. Mandated office return is a temporary regression, not a trend.
