# Problems

> World problems I feel called to address through my work and thinking.

---

## Tool Dependency and Data Imprisonment

Most professional tools trap users' data in proprietary formats and subscription models. When the company changes pricing or shuts down, years of work become inaccessible. This is not acceptable.

The response I am building: tools that use open, human-readable formats (markdown, plain text, SQLite) and run locally. PAI Data UI is a direct answer to this problem.

## Extractive Design Patterns

Attention economies reward interfaces designed to maximize engagement over utility. The result is software that makes people anxious, distracted, and dependent. Good design should do the opposite: accomplish the task and get out of the way.

## Invisible Value in Creative and Knowledge Work

Freelancers and knowledge workers routinely undercharge because their value is invisible. Time logs, project histories, and client outcomes are stored in scattered notes, if at all. Better tooling for tracking and communicating value changes what people can charge.

## Concentration of Infrastructure

Most digital infrastructure runs on three cloud providers. This creates fragility, surveillance risk, and power concentration. The shift toward edge computing, local-first tools, and federated systems is necessary, not just desirable.

## The Craft Gap in Technical Work

As tooling improves and AI assists more work, the floor of technical output rises — but the ceiling drops. Average quality improves while exceptional quality becomes rarer, because fewer people invest in deep craft. I want to occupy and defend the high end.
