# Quotes

> Lines I return to. Collected from books, talks, and people.

---

"The beginning of wisdom is to call things by their proper names." — Confucius

"Design is not how something looks. Design is how something works." — Steve Jobs

"Simplicity is the final achievement. After one has played a vast quantity of notes, it is simplicity that emerges as the crowning reward of art." — Frédéric Chopin

"The factory of the future will have only two employees: a man and a dog. The man will be there to feed the dog. The dog will be there to keep the man from touching the equipment." — Warren Bennis (on automation)

"It is not the strongest of the species that survives, nor the most intelligent — it is the one most adaptable to change." — often attributed to Darwin

"A complex system that works is invariably found to have evolved from a simple system that worked. A complex system designed from scratch never works." — John Gall (Gall's Law)

"Perfection is achieved not when there is nothing more to add, but when there is nothing left to take away." — Antoine de Saint-Exupéry

"The best way to predict the future is to invent it." — Alan Kay

"In theory, theory and practice are the same. In practice, they are not." — attributed to various

"Don't explain your philosophy. Embody it." — Epictetus

"Software is never finished, only abandoned." — anonymous

"Make things as simple as possible, but not simpler." — attributed to Einstein

"What gets measured gets managed." — Peter Drucker (and the danger: what matters often cannot be measured)

"The value of a prototype is in the questions it generates, not in the answers it provides." — unknown
