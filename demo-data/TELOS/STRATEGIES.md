# Strategies

> Active approaches for achieving goals. Reviewed quarterly.

---

## Fewer, Deeper Engagements

Instead of taking many small projects, I focus on 2-3 meaningful client engagements at a time. This allows deeper work, stronger relationships, and better results. The cost is less income stability; the benefit is craft and reputation.

## The Demo as a Sales Tool

PAI Data UI serves double duty: a genuine personal tool and a live demonstration of my technical and design sensibility. Every demo session for a potential client shows the stack I can build for them. Ship quality over quantity.

## Rate Through Positioning, Not Negotiation

I raise my rate by demonstrating value (case studies, referrals, visible work) rather than by negotiating harder. The goal is to attract clients who value the work, not to convince skeptics.

## Build in Public (Selectively)

Publishing technical writing and open source work creates inbound interest without direct sales effort. I document what I build — not to be seen, but because clarity of thought attracts the right collaborators and clients.

## Time-Blocking for Deep Work

Client delivery work (design, code) happens in the morning. Communication, admin, and lighter tasks in the afternoon. No exceptions except travel and client on-sites.

## The Local-First Default

For personal tools and infrastructure, always prefer local-first: data I own, systems I can inspect, dependencies I can remove. PAI Data UI is the flagship of this strategy.
