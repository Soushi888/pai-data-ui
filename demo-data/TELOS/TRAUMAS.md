# Traumas

> Formative difficulties that changed direction or understanding.

---

*This file holds experiences that shaped my work and values — not to dwell on them, but to understand where certain instincts come from.*

---

## The Project That Shipped Without Tests

A client engagement early in my career. We delivered on time and on budget. Six months later, a routine update broke the system and no one could diagnose why — the codebase had no tests, no documentation, and the context lived only in my head.

The client lost trust. I lost sleep. The fix took twice as long as it should have.

**What it left:** An almost irrational commitment to documentation and testability. The feeling that untested, undocumented work is not finished work — it is a liability handed to a future self.

---

## Saying Yes Until I Could Not

A period of taking every project offered, every favour requested, every scope expansion without pushback. I told myself I was building relationships. What I was building was a pattern of exhaustion and resentment that took a long time to recognize and correct.

**What it left:** A healthy instinct to pause before any yes. The question "what am I saying no to in order to say yes to this?" is now reflexive.

---

## A Partnership That Ended Badly

A collaboration with another practitioner that broke down over expectations that were never made explicit. We both assumed we had agreed on scope, ownership, and credit. We had not.

**What it left:** Written agreements before any collaboration, however informal. A standing practice of making assumptions explicit early, at the cost of seeming overly formal.
